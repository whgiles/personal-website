Reliable data systems are built the same way reliable software systems are built: with clear ownership, strong contracts, and explicit operational expectations.

## Start with product thinking

The fastest way to create a fragile pipeline is to treat it like glue code. The better approach is to define the pipeline as a product with users, inputs, outputs, and quality standards.

## Design for recovery

A well-designed pipeline should support replay, idempotent writes, and clear failure boundaries. Recovery should be part of the design, not an afterthought.

## Make quality observable

At minimum, every production pipeline should answer three questions quickly:

1. Did it run?
2. Did it produce the right shape of data?
3. Can it be safely rerun?

That means logging, metrics, validation checkpoints, and operational runbooks should exist from the beginning.

## Why this matters

Strong engineering is visible in the quiet parts of a system: the parts that make it dependable when nobody is watching.
