A system is only as good as its behavior under stress. Operability is the difference between software that looks impressive and software that can be trusted.

## Reduce ambiguity

Operational ambiguity creates long incidents. Systems should emit enough context that an engineer can understand what happened without guessing.

## Bias toward explicit state

When workflows depend on hidden assumptions, production becomes brittle. Explicit states, durable checkpoints, and idempotent operations reduce risk dramatically.

## Build for handoff

The best systems are legible to the next engineer. Naming, documentation, dashboards, and deployment conventions all matter because they reduce cognitive load during change.

## The real goal

Good software is not just software that works. It is software that can keep working while the team and the requirements evolve.
